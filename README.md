👥 HR Analytics Dashboard: Workforce Insights & Employee Attrition Analysis
Recommended Structure and Order
1. Project Title / Headline
A dynamic, interactive Power BI dashboard built to analyze employee workforce data—focusing on employee attrition, workforce demographics, departmental performance, salary distribution, experience trends, and HR key performance indicators to support strategic business decisions.

2. Short Description / Purpose

The HR Analytics Dashboard is a visually engaging and analytical Power BI report designed to help organizations monitor and analyze employee workforce data. The dashboard focuses on key HR metrics such as employee attrition, department-wise employee distribution, salary analysis, gender demographics, age groups, and experience trends.

This dashboard is intended for HR Managers, Business Analysts, Human Resource Teams, Management Executives, and Decision Makers who seek to understand workforce behavior, improve employee retention, and make data-driven HR decisions.

3. Tech Stack

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

📊 Power BI Desktop – Main data visualization platform used for report creation.
📂 Power Query – Data transformation and cleaning layer used for preparing employee data.
🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used for calculated measures, KPIs, dynamic visuals, and business logic.
🔗 Data Modeling – Relationships established among HR tables to enable cross-filtering and interactive reporting.
📈 Interactive Visualizations – KPI Cards, Donut Charts, Bar Charts, Column Charts, Matrix Tables, Line Charts, and Slicers.
📁 File Format – .pbix for dashboard development and .png for dashboard preview.
4. Data Source

Source: HR Employee Dataset (Sample HR Dataset)

The dataset contains employee-related information including employee demographics, department, job role, gender, salary, experience, age, and attrition status. The data has been cleaned, transformed, and modeled to create meaningful KPIs and interactive reports that support workforce analysis and business decision-making.

5. Features / Highlights
• Business Problem

Employee attrition and workforce management are among the biggest challenges faced by organizations. HR departments often rely on spreadsheets and disconnected reports, making it difficult to identify workforce trends and make timely decisions.

Key questions such as:

Which department has the highest employee attrition?
What is the current employee attrition rate?
Which salary slab experiences the highest employee turnover?
How are employees distributed across departments?
What is the average employee age and experience?
Which job roles have the highest attrition?
How does employee experience influence attrition?

...are difficult to answer quickly using raw HR data.

• Goal of the Dashboard

To deliver an interactive HR analytics dashboard that:

Enables organizations to monitor workforce performance in real time.
Helps HR teams identify employee attrition trends.
Supports strategic workforce planning and retention initiatives.
Provides insights into employee demographics and departmental distribution.
Assists business leaders in making informed HR decisions through interactive visualizations.

Attrition by Department (Donut Chart)

Displays employee attrition across various departments, helping HR teams identify departments with higher employee turnover and retention challenges.

Attrition by Salary Slab (Horizontal Bar Chart)

Compares employee attrition across different salary ranges, allowing organizations to understand compensation-related attrition trends.

Job Role Attrition Matrix (Matrix Table)

Provides a detailed breakdown of employee attrition across different job roles, helping identify positions with the highest turnover.

Age Group Distribution (Column Chart)

Visualizes employee distribution across different age groups, enabling HR teams to understand workforce demographics.

Attrition by Gender (Pie Chart)

Shows the proportion of male and female employees who have left the organization, supporting diversity and inclusion analysis.

Attrition Trend by Experience (Line Chart)

Analyzes employee attrition based on years of experience, helping identify trends among new, mid-level, and experienced employees.

Department-wise Employee Count (Bar Chart)

Displays the total number of employees across different departments, providing a clear overview of workforce distribution.

• Business Impact & Insights

Employee Retention: HR teams can identify departments and job roles with high attrition and implement targeted employee retention strategies.

Strategic Workforce Planning: Business leaders can monitor workforce distribution and optimize hiring, staffing, and resource allocation.

Compensation Analysis: Salary-based attrition insights help organizations evaluate compensation structures and improve employee satisfaction.

Performance Monitoring: Interactive KPIs enable continuous monitoring of workforce health and organizational performance.

Data-Driven Decision Making: Decision-makers can explore workforce trends, analyze employee demographics, and make informed HR decisions through interactive reports.
